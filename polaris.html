GROUP OF INTEREST: Polaris Initiative

STATUS: Code Green (Allied/Affiliate)

STANDARD PROCEDURAL POSTURE: All units are clear to cooperate and work jointly with the Initiative as needed, particularly those assigned to Operation STARGAZER

LOCATION: Predominantly based in Antarctica, but with multiple locations across planet Earth and other parts of the Solar System as of [REDACTED]

JOINT ASSETS: Weddell Satellite Network, Byrd Communications Array, Moriusaq Station, Site [REDACTED], McMurdo Station, Belt Defense Array, Kuyper Early Warning System, [REDACTED] Spaceport

DESCRIPTION AND HISTORY:

Initially starting as a clandestine joint program between the United States Antarctic Program and the Federal Republic of Antarctica’s CRUX Bureau as part of Operation Deep Freeze in 1955, the Initiative evolved out of its previously purely scientific role to a hybrid science-military role after the discovery of the remainders of 1940 Germany’s New Swabia expedition beneath the Humboldt Mountains in 1976, resulting in the gradual militarization and expansion of the Initiative’s operations. While the Initiative primarily dedicates itself to the scientific studies around the polar regions, the program later expanded to include space exploration and planetary defense as evidence of extraterrestrial life began to be detected by various satellites and sensory equipment deployed around the planet, particularly in the aftermath of Operations FIRST CONTACT and BEACON.

Contact would be established between the T.S.C. and the Initiative during the former’s initial scouting operations in Greenland in [REDACTED DATE] as part of the organization’s efforts to expand their bases of operations beyond the confines of the mainland North American continent. Upon the discovery of the seemingly abandoned (though in reality, zombie-infested) Moriusaq settlement in the northern part of the territory, Initiative forces stationed at the nearby Pituffik Space Base were deployed to assist the T.S.C. forces in ridding the former settlement of the undead hordes and assisted them in establishing a new station under the protection and funding of the Initiative. As a result of this initial interaction, cooperation and growth between the two organizations has increased over the years, with much of the current funding and manpower from the T.S.C. coming as a result of contributions from the Initiative’s liaisons in the United States and Antarctica. In return, the T.S.C. was able to contribute to the subsequent developments of the Weddell Satellite Network and Byrd Communications Array as part of the joint American-Antarctic space programs.

As of the present date, the most recent (and notable) joint operation between the T.S.C. and the Initiative has been with Operation STARGAZER, which currently handles the affairs involving the overall defense of Earth and the Solar System along with the investigation and search for extraterrestrial life within our local stellar neighborhood. Established in the aftermath of the Operation FIRST CONTACT and BEACON Incidents as of 03/15/2025, Operation STARGAZER currently consists of joint T.S.C.- Initiative Units* stationed in various bases scattered across both organizations bases across planet Earth and the Solar System, with various assets specializing in fields ranging from scientific research to weapons development. The most notable developments so far in the operation have been the Belt Defense Array and the Kuyper Early Warning System, a developing series of defense arrays stationed across the Asteroid and Kuyper Belts surrounding the Solar System serving to act as a front line of defense as needed in the event of an extrasolar invasion. Construction and development is currently in progress and will be completed by [REDACTED DATE].

*Addendum 05/05/2025: Per the recent events coming from the current administration, funding and affairs of the Polaris Initiative have been indefinitely transferred to the Antarctic branch. The United States Antarctic Program will continue to collaborate with CRUX as part of the Initiative, but will likely be reliant on funding from the latter to operate due to uncertainty from the part of the United States government. Further updates will follow as more information is released.
